About me and my interests


I first got close to programming in highschool (~2017) after taking a course on game development with GML in GameMaker Studio.

I then spent two years taking courses on video game programming with Unity and C#, while participating in multiple game jams with friends. Some of the resulting games (The Key, Space Rewinders, Frank) are listed on the section above!

Throughout my final year of highschool and first two years of university (2020-2022), I worked as a freelance game developer, providing complete solutions for clients all around the world.

Working on real-life, professional projects with Unity and C# allowed me to learn more about good coding practices, especially SOLID principles and design patterns in OOP.
I also learned a lot about designing products that people like using.

For the past year (2022 to 2023) I've been focusing solely on school. I'm pursuing a bachelor's degree in computer science at FaMAF, UNC - taking lots of cool courses and working on cool projects :)

Some of these include building a small graphics renderer for a Raspberry PI in bare ARMv8 assembler, writing a scheduler and messing with the kernel of xv6 (a UNIX-based OS), building a filesystem, and doing (lots) of math and classic algorithms + data structures, mainly in C.

On July this year (2023), I was awarded a small scolarship to participate in the ECI 2023, a yearly event that takes place at the University of Buenos Aires. There, I learned about using fuzzing for compiler testing, and about complementary ways of leveraging AI to improve prediction tasks.

Right now, I'm particularly interested in machine learning, exploring practical applications of LLMs, learning how operating systems work, learning web app development, and lots of other things!
I'm looking forward to 2024 to keep working on personal projects and releasing stuff I've been working on over this year :)
